Macaroon

Price: R6

Serving size: 30


Macarons are delicate, meringue-based cookies made from almond flour, egg whites, and sugar. They are known for their smooth, domed tops, ruffled edges (called “feet”), and a variety of fillings like ganache, buttercream, or jam. These cookies are often brightly colored and come in a wide range of flavors.

Steps

  1. Prepare the dry ingredients:Sift the almond flour and powdered sugar together to remove any lumps. This ensures a smooth macaron shell.
  2. Make the meringue:Beat the egg whites with cream of tartar until they become frothy.
  3. Gradually add the granulated sugar while continuing to beat until stiff peaks form. If you want colored macarons, add the food coloring at this stage.
  4. Combine the ingredients:Gently fold the almond flour mixture into the meringue. Be careful not to overmix; the batter should flow like lava and form a ribbon when lifted.
  5. Pipe the macarons:Transfer the batter to a piping bag fitted with a round tip. Pipe small circles onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.Tap the baking sheet on the counter to release any air bubbles.
  6. Rest the macarons:Let the piped macarons sit at room temperature for 30-60 minutes until they form a skin. This helps them develop their characteristic “feet” during baking.
  7. Bake:Preheat your oven to 150°C (300°F). Bake the macarons for 15-18 minutes. They should have risen and developed feet.
  8. Cool and fill:Allow the macarons to cool completely on the baking sheet before removing them.Once cooled, pair the shells by size and fill them with your chosen filling.

Extra Notes/Requests:

  1. Flavors: Experiment with different flavors like matcha, chocolate, raspberry, or lemon.
  2. Colors: Use gel food coloring to achieve vibrant colors without altering the batter consistency.
